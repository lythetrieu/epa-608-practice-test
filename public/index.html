Everything You Need to Know About EPA 608

What is the EPA 608 certification and who needs it?
A federal certification mandated under Section 608 of the Clean Air Act for any HVAC technician who services, repairs, or disposes of appliances containing regulated refrigerants. The exam covers Core regulations plus up to three equipment-specific sections (Type I, II, III). Content reflects 2026 AIM Act updates including HFC phasedown requirements. If you're currently in an HVAC program, see our guide for HVAC students for study sequencing advice tailored to apprentices.
How much does the real EPA 608 exam cost?
The official Section 608 exam costs $10–$40 per attempt depending on the testing organization. Core-only is typically the lowest cost; Universal (all four sections) costs the most. Retakes require paying the full fee again — which is why our Pro $14.99 lifetime makes sense: pass the first time.

How long should I study before the real exam?
Most HVAC technicians need 1–3 weeks of focused study. Beginners typically need 3–5 weeks. Experienced techs already familiar with refrigerant systems may only need a week. The readiness signal: scoring 85%+ consistently across 3+ practice sessions. The real passing threshold is 72%.
Does EPA 608 certification expire?
No — Section 608 Technician Certification is a lifetime credential valid permanently once earned. Keep the physical card as proof. Regulations change (the 2026 AIM Act added new HFC requirements), so stay current with industry updates — but the credential itself never expires.
What's the difference between EPA 608 and 609?
Section 608 covers stationary equipment — buildings, commercial systems, industrial chillers, residential HVAC. Section 609 covers mobile vehicle air conditioning (MVAC — cars, trucks). Both are federal mandates under the Clean Air Act but apply to different equipment. Technicians working on both need both certifications.
