EPA 608 Exam Day: What to Expect & How to Prepare

The EPA 608 exam is a closed-book, proctored test administered by an EPA-approved testing organization (ATO) like ESCO Institute or HVAC Excellence. Here is exactly what happens on test day.

What to Bring

  • Government-issued photo ID — driver's license, passport, or state ID
  • Pencils or pens — some test centers provide them, bring your own to be safe
  • Payment — exam fee ranges from $20 to $40 depending on the ATO and location
  • Confirmation email or registration number — if you registered online

You do NOT need to bring your own calculator, reference materials, or tools. The exam is closed-book.

Test Format

Section Questions Passing Score
Core2570% (18/25)
Type I2584% (21/25)*
Type II2570% (18/25)
Type III2570% (18/25)
Universal100 (all 4)Pass each section

*Type I has a higher passing score (84%) because it is an open-book section on the actual EPA exam.

Timing

  • Each section: approximately 30 minutes
  • Universal (all 4 sections): approximately 2 hours
  • Most technicians finish in 45-60 minutes for a single section
  • There is no penalty for guessing — answer every question

Scoring & Results

  • Results are typically available immediately or within a few days
  • You must pass Core plus at least one Type to earn any certification
  • If you fail a section, you can retake it — but you pay the exam fee again
  • Your certification card is mailed within 2-4 weeks after passing
  • EPA 608 certification does not expire — it is valid for life

Tips from Technicians Who Passed First Try

  1. Take practice tests until you consistently score 85%+ — the real exam has trick questions
  2. Memorize the numbers — evacuation levels, leak rates, fine amounts, pressure categories
  3. Know the differences — recovery vs recycling vs reclamation, ODP vs GWP, CFC vs HCFC vs HFC
  4. Read every option carefully — wrong answers are designed to sound almost right
  5. Don't rush — you have plenty of time, double-check flagged questions
