# BRAND-GUIDE.md — EPA 608 Practice Test Voice & Style

## QUICK REFERENCE

**Brand:** EPA 608 Practice Test
**Tagline:** "Pass your EPA 608. Free tools. No BS."
**Voice:** Senior HVAC tech who helps you pass — direct, confident, no fluff.
**Audience:** HVAC technicians, apprentices, career changers (blue collar, trade workers)

---

## VOICE RULES

### Always
- Short sentences. Subject-verb-object.
- Specific numbers: "25 questions" not "comprehensive assessment"
- Action verbs: pass, fix, practice, study, score, earn
- Real comparisons: "costs less than lunch" not "affordable investment"
- Respect intelligence: they know refrigerants, they need to know the rules

### Never
- Corporate speak: "leverage", "unlock potential", "seamless experience"
- Condescending: "even beginners can understand", "simple enough for anyone"
- Vague promises: "boost your confidence", "take your career to the next level"
- Fake urgency: "only 3 spots left!", "limited time!" (unless actually true)
- Emoji in headings or body copy (OK in social media only)

---

## COPY FORMULAS

### Headlines
```
[Benefit] + [Specificity] + [No catch]
"25 Free EPA 608 Questions. Instant Score. No Signup."
```

### CTAs
```
[Action verb] + [What they get]
"Start Practicing" | "See My Weak Spots" | "Get Pro — $18.99"
```

### Feature Descriptions
```
[What it does] + [Why you care] — 1 sentence max
"Blind-spot training — we find what you don't know, then drill it."
```

### Objection Handling
```
"Is it really free?" → "Yes. 25 questions, all 5 sections, unlimited. No credit card."
"Why should I pay?" → "Free gets you ready. Pro tells you exactly where you're weak and guarantees you pass."
"Is $18.99 worth it?" → "That's 45 minutes of tech pay. And if you don't pass, you get it back."
```

---

## FOMO RULES

### Real FOMO only (use data from platform)
- "X technicians practiced this week" (real counter from DB)
- "Exam season: most techs study January–March" (fact)
- "$18.99 now. Price increases as we add features." (honest)

### Never fake
- "Only 3 spots left!" (no spots exist)
- "50% off today only!" (price never changes)
- Countdown timers with no real deadline

## VISUAL STYLE

### Colors
```
Primary:     #1e40af (deep blue — trust)
Success:     #16a34a (green — pass/correct)
Error:       #dc2626 (red — fail/wrong)
Background:  #f8fafc → #ffffff
Text:        #0f172a (near-black)
Secondary:   #64748b (gray-500)
```

### Typography
```
Font:        Inter (Google Fonts)
Headlines:   Bold, 24-32px, short sentences
Body:        Regular, 16-18px, line-height 1.6+
Min size:    12px (text-xs) — never smaller
```

### Buttons
```
Primary:   #1e40af bg, white text, rounded-xl, min-h 48px
Secondary: border only, gray text, rounded-xl
No: gradients, shadows, animations
```

### Cards
```
White bg, 1px border gray-200, rounded-xl
No: drop shadows, gradients, decorative borders
```

## AUDIENCE CONTEXT

- HVAC techs use phones (70% Android), study on breaks/in vans
- They earn $25-45/hr — $18.99 = under 1 hour of work
- They respect straight talk, distrust marketing hype
- They trust peer recommendations over brand promises
- EPA 608 study takes 3-5 weeks — they need tools that work fast
- Many are immigrants or career changers — accessibility matters
