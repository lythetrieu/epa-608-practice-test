Online Testing: The New Standard

By 2026, most technicians are choosing the online proctored route. Organizations like SkillCat and Mainstream Engineering have revolutionized the process by allowing you to take the exam using your laptop's webcam. The AI-driven proctoring software monitors your eye movements and surroundings to ensure academic integrity.

Where to Find In-Person Testing

If you prefer a traditional classroom environment, you can typically find EPA-approved testing at:

  • Supply Houses: Many HVAC wholesalers host quarterly testing events.
  • Trade Schools: Local community colleges and vocational centers often have open proctoring dates.
  • Local Unions: UA (United Association) halls frequently certify their members and associates.

Provider Comparison (2026)

The most recognized EPA-authorized testing organizations include:

  • ESCO Institute: The industry leader in technical certification.
  • SkillCat: Popular for their mobile-first training and fast online testing.
  • VGI: Known for their complete study manuals and paper exams.

The Type I "Open-Book" Fact

Many technicians don't know this: Type I (Small Appliances) is frequently administered as an open-book exam at in-person testing centers. This does not apply to online proctored sessions, where all sections are closed-book. If you plan to test in person and only need Type I certification, ask your proctor in advance about their open-book policy — it can significantly reduce preparation time.
