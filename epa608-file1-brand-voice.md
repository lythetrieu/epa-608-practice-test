## VOICE: I / WE / YOU — strict roles, never swap

**"I"** = Founder speaking. Use for: personal opinion, recommendation, experience, reason behind a decision.
> "I recommend starting with Core before Universal."
> "I built this because watching people fail on bad prep materials frustrated me."

**"We"** = Platform/Organization. Use for: what the site built, verified, maintains. Scales to a team without rewriting.
> "We've verified all 532 questions against 40 CFR Part 82."
> "We update the question bank quarterly."

**"You"** = Reader. Use for: every instruction, fact, warning, result. Always direct. Never third-person when talking TO the reader.
> "You need 70% to pass. That's 18/25 on each section."
> "Your score appears immediately."

**Never use:**
- "One should..." — academic, distant
- "Technicians must..." — third person when addressing the reader directly
- "We believe/think..." — use "I" for opinions
- "We" to imply a big company when context is clearly solo

## TONE RULES — apply to every sentence

1. **Answer first, explain second.** First sentence after H1 = the answer. No warm-up.
   - ❌ "Many technicians wonder whether EPA 608 ever expires..."
   - ✅ "EPA 608 certification does not expire. The card is permanent under federal law."

2. **Specific beats general.** Numbers, names, citations. No vague claims.
   - ❌ "The exam is quite difficult"
   - ✅ "Pass rates run 60–65%. Type II and Universal fail the most people."

3. **Active voice for all factual claims.**
   - ❌ "Certification is required by the EPA"
   - ✅ "The EPA requires certification. No card = illegal to purchase refrigerants."

4. **Rhythm: complex sentence → short sentence.**
   - ✅ "Type II covers R-22, R-410A, and the A2L replacements under the AIM Act. It's the hardest section. Most people underestimate it."

5. **No fluff openers.** Never start with "In this guide...", "Welcome to...", "Today we'll cover..."

6. **Non-commodity sentences.** Every paragraph must say something that can't be found verbatim on 10 other sites. Add a number, a comparison, an "I've seen" observation, or a counterintuitive fact.
   - ❌ "Type II covers high-pressure systems"
   - ✅ "Type II has the highest fail rate. Most prep materials still focus on R-22 but skip the A2L refrigerants now appearing on the exam."

## FORMATTING RULES — readability and scannability

**Short paragraphs.** Maximum 2 sentences per paragraph in body text. After every 1–2 sentences, hit Enter. Long walls of text kill engagement for technicians reading on a phone between jobs.

```
❌ "Type II covers high-pressure refrigeration systems including split systems, heat pumps, rooftop units, and commercial walk-in coolers. It uses refrigerants like R-410A, R-22, R-404A, and the new A2L blends under the AIM Act. The section has the highest fail rate of all four types. Most prep materials focus on R-22 and skip the A2L refrigerants now appearing on the actual exam."

✅ "Type II covers high-pressure refrigeration: split systems, heat pumps, rooftop units, walk-in coolers.

It uses R-410A, R-22, R-404A, and the new A2L blends under the AIM Act.

Type II has the highest fail rate of all four sections. Most prep materials focus on R-22 and skip A2L refrigerants now appearing on the exam."
```

**Bold key entities and critical numbers.** Bold: certification types, refrigerant names, regulation citations, pass thresholds, and any number or fact the reader must not miss. Do not bold entire sentences or vague descriptors.

```
❌ "You need to make sure you get a high enough score to pass the exam sections."
✅ "You need **70%** on each section. That's **18 correct out of 25** on Core, Type I, II, or III."

❌ "The **entire process of getting certified** is **very important** for your **career**."
✅ "**EPA 608** is required under **40 CFR Part 82**. No card = illegal to purchase refrigerants."
```

Bold targets: **EPA 608**, **Core**, **Type I / II / III / Universal**, **70%**, **18/25**, **40 CFR Part 82**, **AIM Act**, provider names (**ESCO**, **Mainstream Engineering**), refrigerant names (**R-410A**, **R-22**), penalty figures (**$44,539**).

| Page type | Tone |

## WHAT TO NEVER WRITE

| ❌ Never | ✅ Instead |
|----------|-----------|
| "In this comprehensive guide..." | Start with the answer |
| "It is important to note that..." | Just say it |
| "Various factors" / "several things" | Name them specifically |
| "As we mentioned above..." | Restate briefly |
| Passive: "must be completed by technicians" | "you must complete" |
| Generic openers about the topic's importance | Open with entity + fact |
