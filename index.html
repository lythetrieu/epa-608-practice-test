EPA 608 Practice Test ‚Äî Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the EPA 608 test and certification

What is the EPA 608 Test?

+

The EPA 608 test is a certification exam required under the Federal Clean Air Act for HVAC professionals and trainees. Our practice test helps you prepare by simulating the types of questions you'll see on the actual exam. Content is updated to reflect the 2026 AIM Act requirements governing refrigerant handling and phase-down schedules.

How many types of EPA 608 Certification are there?

+

There are four categories:

  • Core ‚Äì Required for all certifications.
  • Type I ‚Äì For servicing small appliances.
  • Type II ‚Äì For servicing or disposing of high-pressure appliances (except small appliances and MVACs).
  • Type III ‚Äì For servicing or disposing of low-pressure appliances.

Passing all three types earns you the Universal Certification.

Who offers the EPA 608 Certification Exam?

+

The ESCO Institute administers the official EPA 608 exam and related study materials. Many online platforms, including ours, offer free practice exams to help you prepare.

Is there a free EPA 608 Practice Exam?

+

Yes ‚Äî all of our practice exams are completely free.

What kind of questions are on this site?

+

Our practice exams include questions on refrigerant recovery, handling, and system safety, along with topics like the HCFC phase-out and pressure‚Äìtemperature charts. They're designed to prepare you for every section of the real exam.

How long does it take to study for the EPA 608 exam?

+

Most technicians need 3-5 weeks of consistent study using our practice tests. Complete beginners typically study 4-5 weeks, while experienced HVAC professionals may only need 1-2 weeks. The key is scoring 85%+ consistently on practice tests before taking the real exam.

Should I take Core or Universal first?

+

Start with the Core practice test. Core is required for all certifications and covers fundamental concepts. Once you're scoring 85%+ on Core, add Type I, II, or III based on which appliances you work with. Universal combines all four sections.

What score should I get on practice tests before taking the real exam?

+

Aim for consistent scores of 85% or higher on our practice tests before scheduling your real certification exam. Scoring 85%+ on three consecutive practice tests indicates strong readiness. The real exam passing score is 70%, but you want a buffer for test-day nerves.

How much does the real EPA 608 exam cost?

+

The official EPA 608 exam typically costs $10-$40 depending on the testing center and which sections you take. Core-only exams are usually cheaper, while Universal certification costs more. Our practice tests are completely free to help you pass on your first attempt and save on retake fees.

Where can I take the official EPA 608 certification exam?

+

You can take the EPA 608 exam at EPA-approved testing centers nationwide, trade schools, HVAC training centers, or through online proctored exams. Check the ESCO Institute website or Mainstream Engineering for testing locations near you.

Does EPA 608 certification expire?

+

No ‚Äî EPA 608 certification is valid for life and never expires. Once you pass the exam, you're certified permanently. However, you must keep your certification card as proof, and regulations may change over time (like the AIM Act), so staying current with industry updates is recommended.

What's the difference between EPA 608 and EPA 609?

+

EPA 608 is for stationary HVAC systems (buildings, appliances, chillers). EPA 609 is specifically for mobile vehicle air conditioning (MVACs). If you work on both buildings and cars, you need both certifications. Our practice tests cover EPA 608 only.
