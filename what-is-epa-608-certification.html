What is EPA 608 Certification?

Your complete roadmap to earning the mandatory federal technician license for HVAC professionals.

If you intend to work on air conditioning and refrigeration systems in the United States, obtaining an EPA Section 608 Technician Certification is not optional—it is a federal requirement. Mandated by the Clean Air Act, this certification ensures that technicians know how to safely handle and recover refrigerants that can deplete the ozone layer or contribute to global warming.

The Four Types of Certification

The EPA 608 exam is divided into four technical sections. To earn a certification, you must first pass the Core Section, and then any of the following:

  • Type I: For servicing small appliances (5 lbs or less of refrigerant).
  • Type II: For servicing high-pressure systems (residential AC, split systems, heat pumps).
  • Type III: For servicing low-pressure systems (centrifugal chillers).
  • Universal: Awarded if you pass all four sections (Core, I, II, and III).

Why Is This Mandatory?

Under the Clean Air Act (Section 608), the EPA mandates certification for any technician who handles refrigerants. Refrigerants like CFCs and HCFCs contain chlorine, which destroys the Earth's stratospheric ozone layer. Even newer HFCs (like R-410A) are potent greenhouse gases. The Section 608 program ensures only trained professionals can buy or vent these chemicals.

How to Get Certified (Step-by-Step)

  1. Choose Your Path: Decide if you need Type I, II, III, or the Universal certification based on your job role.
  2. Study the Materials: Focus on leak rates, recovery standards, and safety procedures. (Use our Free Study Guides).
  3. Practice the Exam: Take multiple mock tests to ensure you hit at least 80% consistently.
  4. Schedule the Proctor: Find an EPA-approved testing center or an online proctored service.
  5. Pass and Receive Card: Most providers issue a physical wallet card and a digital certificate.

Passing Score and Difficulty

To pass any section, you must score at least 72%. On the typical 25-question battery, that means you can only miss 7 questions. The Core section is often considered the most difficult because it involves legal statutes and chemistry facts that aren't used in daily field work.

2026 Updates: The AIM Act and HFC Phase-Down

The American Innovation and Manufacturing (AIM) Act of 2020 now directly affects EPA 608 certification content. Current exams include questions on the phased reduction of HFCs — particularly R-410A — and the transition to lower-GWP A2L refrigerants such as R-32 and R-454B. Technicians must understand flammable refrigerant safety classifications (A2L = mildly flammable) and updated handling requirements. Our 2026 study guides are updated to reflect these changes.

Certification vs. License — What's the Difference?

EPA 608 certification is a federal credential issued by EPA-approved organizations. It is not a state license. Many states require additional contractor licenses to operate a business, but the EPA 608 certification itself is nationally recognized and has no expiration date — for a full breakdown of validity rules and what happens if you lose your card, see our EPA 608 certification validity and expiration rules guide. A certified technician can legally purchase refrigerant from any EPA-registered distributor in any state.

Note that EPA 608 and EPA 609 are two separate federal certifications covering different refrigerant systems. If you work on mobile air conditioning (vehicles), you may also need an EPA 609 certification — see the full EPA 608 vs 609 comparison to understand which applies to your work.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does EPA 608 certification expire?

No. Once earned, your Section 608 certification is valid for life. There is no renewal requirement under current EPA regulations. For details on lost cards, revocation scenarios, and record verification, see the EPA 608 Practice Test certification validity rules guide.

Can I buy refrigerant without certification?

No. Purchasing refrigerant in containers over 2 lbs requires valid Section 608 certification. Distributors are required by law to verify credentials before sale.

What is the passing score for the EPA 608 exam?

You must answer at least 18 out of 25 questions correctly (70% minimum) in each section. The Core section is widely considered the most difficult due to its focus on environmental regulations and legal statutes.

Who are the EPA-approved testing organizations?

ESCO Institute, Mainstream Engineering, and the National Refrigerants Recertification Program are among the most widely used. See our full testing options guide for current providers.
