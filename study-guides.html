How the EPA 608 Exam Works ‚Äî EPA 608 Practice Test

Before diving into section-specific study guides, understand how the exam is structured. The EPA Section 608 certification exam is divided into four sections: Core, Type I, Type II, and Type III. Each section contains 25 questions for a total of 100 questions if you are testing for Universal certification. You must score at least 70% (18 of 25 correct) on each section to pass under standard proctored conditions. Prepare with an EPA 608 Practice Test before sitting for the proctored exam to ensure you meet that threshold on every section.

Universal certification means passing all four sections. Once earned, your EPA 608 certification never expires ‚Äî there is no recertification requirement. However, the legal stakes for working without certification are significant: handling refrigerants without a valid Section 608 certification violates the Federal Clean Air Act, with fines up to $44,539 per violation as of 2026. Review the full certification requirements before scheduling your exam.

Exam Structure and Passing Scores

Each of the four sections ‚Äî Core, Type I, Type II, and Type III ‚Äî consists of 25 multiple-choice questions. The minimum passing score for proctored exams is 70% (18 correct answers). There is no penalty for guessing, so answer every question. Two EPA-approved testing agencies administer the exam: ESCO Institute and Mainstream Engineering. Both follow the same EPA-mandated content areas and passing thresholds.

Proctored vs. Non-Proctored Rules

The Core section must always be taken as a proctored (closed-book) exam ‚Äî no exceptions. Type I has a non-proctored option: technicians can take the Type I exam open-book, but the passing threshold increases to 84% (21 of 25 correct) under non-proctored conditions. Type II and Type III are proctored only. Check with your testing center to confirm which format is available for your exam session and review important regulatory dates that may affect test content.

What to Do If You've Already Failed the EPA 608 Exam

Failing one or more sections does not erase your progress. Partial certification is preserved ‚Äî you only need to retake the specific section or sections you failed. However, there is one critical exception: if you fail the Core section, no Type certifications are awarded regardless of your scores on Type I, II, or III. You must pass Core before any type certifications count.

Retake fees are charged per attempt and vary by testing center. Do I need to study all four sections to get Universal?

Yes. Universal certification requires passing all four sections (Core + Type I + II + III) with a minimum 70% score on each. There is no shortcut ‚Äî all four must be passed.

Are the 2026 AIM Act changes on the exam?

Yes. Current proctored exams include questions on HFC phase-down, A2L refrigerant classifications, and updated GWP standards. Our study guides and practice tests are updated to reflect these changes.

What is the hardest section of the EPA 608 exam?

Type II is most commonly cited due to the volume of specific numeric thresholds ‚Äî leak rates, vacuum levels, and 50-pound system rules. Type III is the most specialized but has fewer test-takers, so study material is less common. Our Type II study guide covers all testable thresholds.

Do I have to study all four sections at the same time for Universal?

No. You can take EPA 608 exam sections in any order and certify incrementally. Many technicians pass Core and Type II first, then add Type I and Type III later. All four must be passed to earn Universal certification, but there is no requirement to take them simultaneously.

Which testing agency is better ‚Äî ESCO or Mainstream Engineering?

Both ESCO Institute and Mainstream Engineering administer EPA-approved Section 608 exams with the same passing requirements. Neither is better ‚Äî choose based on which agency your local testing center uses. Our EPA 608 Practice Test covers content tested by both agencies.

What is the passing score for the non-proctored Type I exam?

The non-proctored (open-book) Type I exam requires a minimum score of 84% (21 of 25 correct), compared to 70% for proctored exams. Core must always be proctored regardless of Type I testing format.
