2026 Regulatory Updates ‚Äî The AIM Act and HFC Phase-Down

The American Innovation and Manufacturing (AIM) Act of 2020 mandates a phased reduction in HFC production and consumption in the United States. This directly impacts technicians working with R-410A, R-134a, and other high-GWP refrigerants. Current EPA 608 exams now include AIM Act questions ‚Äî particularly around the transition to lower-GWP alternatives and A2L (mildly flammable) refrigerant safety handling.

How long does it take to study for the EPA 608 exam?

Most technicians with field experience pass within 1‚Äì2 weeks of focused study. Entry-level candidates typically need 3‚Äì4 weeks. Our modular guides let you focus only on your weak sections to reduce total study time.

Do I need to study all four sections to get Universal?

Yes. Universal certification requires passing all four sections (Core + Type I + II + III) with a minimum 70% score on each. There is no shortcut ‚Äî all four must be passed.

Are the 2026 AIM Act changes on the exam?

Yes. Current proctored exams include questions on HFC phase-down, A2L refrigerant classifications, and updated GWP standards. Our study guides and practice tests are updated to reflect these changes.

What is the hardest section of the EPA 608 exam?

Type II is most commonly cited due to the volume of specific numeric thresholds ‚Äî leak rates, vacuum levels, and 50-pound system rules. Type III is the most specialized but has fewer test-takers, so study material is less common. Our Type II study guide covers all testable thresholds.
