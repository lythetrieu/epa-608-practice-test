EPA 608 Practice Test: How to Memorize These Numbers in 24 Hours

  • 10-20-30 Rule: Comfort = 10%, Commercial = 20%, IPR = 30%. Think "residential is strictest."
  • 15-10 Rule: Type III rupture disc = 15 psig. Max test pressure = 10 psig. Never confuse them.
  • 80-90 Rule: Type I recovery — 90% working compressor, 80% non-working.
  • Three Rs: Recovery (remove), Recycling (clean on-site), Reclamation (ARI 700 standard). Know the definitions cold.

Frequently Asked Questions

What numbers do I need to memorize for the EPA 608 exam?

The critical numbers: 70% passing score (18/25). Leak rates: 10/20/30% by system type. Type I recovery: 90% working, 80% non-working. Type II HCFC vacuums: 4" Hg (small), 10–15" Hg (large). R-410A recovery: 0 psig. Type III vacuum: 25" Hg. Rupture disc: 15 psig. Max nitrogen test: 10 psig.

What are the cylinder color codes?

Key codes: R-22 = Green, R-410A = Pink/Rose, R-134a = Light Blue, R-404A = Orange, R-32 = Blue, R-407C = Burnt Orange, R-454B = Light Green. Recovery cylinders are always Gray body with Yellow top and may only contain recovered refrigerant.

What is the difference between recover, recycle, and reclaim?

Recover = remove refrigerant from system without processing it (field). Recycle = clean on-site using oil separation and filtration — stays with same owner. Reclaim = process to ARI 700 standards at EPA-certified facility — the only process that produces reusable-by-anyone refrigerant.

What is the venting penalty under the Clean Air Act?

Civil penalties up to $44,539 per day per violation for knowingly venting refrigerant. Criminal penalties (fines + imprisonment) are also possible. De minimis releases during good-faith recovery are exempt. Any intentional release — including "just checking pressure" — is a violation.

When was R-22 phased out and what replaced it?

R-22 production and import were banned January 1, 2020. Only reclaimed or recovered R-22 can be used for existing systems. R-410A became the main replacement but is now also being phased down under the AIM Act. R-454B and R-32 (both A2L class) are the next-generation replacements for residential AC.

What are ASHRAE safety groups and why do they matter?

ASHRAE 34 safety groups rate refrigerant toxicity (A=low, B=high) and flammability (1=none, 2L=mildly flammable, 2=flammable, 3=highly flammable). They appear on EPA 608 Core and Type II exam questions. Common safe options: A1 (R-22, R-410A, R-134a). Common A2L options now in use: R-32, R-454B, R-1234yf.
