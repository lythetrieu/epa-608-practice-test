Chronological Regulatory Timeline

  • 1987

    The Montreal Protocol is Signed

    This international treaty was signed to protect the stratospheric ozone layer by phasing out the production of ozone-depleting substances (ODS) like CFCs and HCFCs.

  • 1990

    Clean Air Act Amendments

    The United States Congress amended the Clean Air Act, establishing Section 608 which mandates technician certification and recovery of refrigerants.

  • July 1, 1992

    Mandatory Venting Ban

    It became illegal to knowingly vent CFC and HCFC refrigerants into the atmosphere during service, repair, or disposal of HVAC equipment.

  • 1996

    CFC Production Ban

    The production and import of CFC refrigerants (like R-12) was completely banned in the United States under the Montreal Protocol phaseout schedule.

  • Jan 1, 2020

    HCFC-22 Production Ban

    The manufacture and import of virgin R-22 and R-142b was banned. Existing systems must rely on reclaimed or recycled refrigerant.

  • Jan 1, 2025

    AIM Act GWP Restrictions

    New regulations under the AIM Act began restricting the use of high-GWP HFCs (like R-410A) in new residential and light commercial equipment.

Memory Techniques for Regulatory Milestones

Technicians often struggle with the difference between 1987 and 1996. A simple way to remember is that Montreal (1987) was the promise, while the 1996 Ban was the punishment for CFC production.

  • 1992 vs 1995: Venting became illegal first (1992), and technician certification followed shortly after (1995).
